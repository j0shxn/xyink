As per convention we express unitary vectors in $\mathbb{R}^3$ with a hat.

\begin{equation}
    \hat w = \begin{bmatrix}
            \cos(\lambda) \\
            \sin(\lambda) \\
            0
        \end{bmatrix} , \;
    \;\;\;
    \hat p := \begin{bmatrix}
        \cos(\lambda) \cos(\gamma) \\
        \sin(\lambda) \cos(\gamma) \\
        \sin(\gamma)
    \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}

We define the surface of a joint as being the set of all unitary vectors where
their dot products against the joint axis are some value $c$.
\begin{equation} 
    c \in [-1,1], \mathbb{S}_{\hat k}(c) = \{ \hat v :
        \hat v \cdot \hat k = c \}.
\end{equation}
For any rotation around the joint axis the dot products of vectors belonging
to the surface are conserved:
\begin{equation}
    \forall \hat v \in \mathbb{S}_{\hat k}(c), \forall \theta \in (-\pi,\pi],
        R_{\hat k}(\theta)\hat v \in \mathbb{S}_{\hat k}(c)
        \because R_{\hat k}(\theta) \in SO(3).
\end{equation}
We define the concept of loose reachability as satisfying the below condition,
\begin{align}
        \hat l \in \mathbb{S}_{\hat x} (\hat l \cdot \hat x) & \therefore 
        R_{\hat x} (\psi) R_{\hat w}(\phi) \hat p = \hat l \\
        & \Leftrightarrow R_{\hat w}(\phi) \hat p 
        \in \mathbb{S}_{\hat x}(\hat l \cdot \hat x) \\
        & \Leftrightarrow R_{\hat w} (\phi) \hat p \cdot \hat x = 
        \hat l \cdot \hat x.
\end{align}
As such if there is a $\phi$ angle that can bring $\hat p$ to the surface
of $\hat x$ defined by $\hat l$ we consider $\hat l$ to be loosely reachable.
Solving Eq(5) for $\phi$ we get:

        \frac{ \cos(\alpha) \cos(\beta) - \cos(\gamma) \cos(\lambda) }
        { \sin(\gamma) \sin(\lambda) }
    \right]
\end{equation}
